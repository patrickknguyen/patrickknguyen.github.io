Project Objective

ZipVote is a voter-candidate matching tool designed to help first-time voters navigate the ballot with confidence. Users enter their zip code, complete a short policy preference quiz, and receive a ranked breakdown of local candidates based on alignment — covering multiple simultaneous races from a single session.

Project Overview

ZipVote uses a multi-agent AI pipeline to research local candidates automatically and generate personalized quiz questions tailored to each race. A Likert-scale input captures preference intensity rather than binary positions, and a wedge-based scoring system surfaces the distinctions between candidates that are most likely to drive a voter's decision.

1 / Background & Motivation

Voter guides tend to be either overwhelming or too generic to be actionable, particularly for first-time voters approaching a midterm ballot. The 2026 elections presented a concrete opportunity to address this: a high-stakes cycle with a large cohort of eligible first-time voters, many of whom face multiple unfamiliar races simultaneously.

2 / Ballot Scope & Personalization Challenge

A ballot in Somerville, MA looks nothing like one in Phoenix, AZ — candidates, parties, and the fault lines between them are entirely local. Static content cannot scale to that variability. ZipVote is designed around zip-code-level resolution, pulling race-specific candidate data and generating questions that reflect the actual distinctions within each contest.

3 / User Experience & The Likert Scale

Users are presented with a series of policy statements and asked to rate their agreement on a five-point scale, from strongly disagree to strongly agree. This captures preference intensity rather than a simple yes/no — an important distinction when two candidates both claim the same position but differ in emphasis. The result is a ranked candidate list with a per-race breakdown.

4 / Agent Architecture & Wedge Scoring

Scout Agent

A Scout agent builds candidate profiles from Ballotpedia and Wikipedia.

Wedge Agent

A Wedge agent then reads those profiles and identifies the specific points of disagreement between candidates in the same race — rather than scoring against a generic ideological axis. This ensures the quiz questions are the ones that actually differentiate the candidates a voter is choosing between.

5 / Outcomes & Learnings

The current prototype covers the 2026 Massachusetts Senate Democratic primary for a single zip code. The pipeline generalizes by design; the primary remaining work is accuracy validation of AI-generated candidate profiles and usability testing with first-time voters. The wedge-over-ideology framing proved especially valuable in primaries, where standard left/right positioning offers limited signal.
