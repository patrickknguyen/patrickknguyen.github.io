Project Objective

To optimize the operative note generation pipeline by refining large language model prompting strategies, utilizing multi-modal data to accurately reconstruct surgical events.

Project Overview

ViGOR explores the intersection of computer vision and clinical documentation. By parsing surgical video streams, we engineered a system that converts pixel data into structured textual metadata, enabling LLMs to generate highly accurate medical reports with reduced hallucination rates.

1 / Problem Space

Improving Clinical Accuracy

Currently, operative notes are dictated from memory long after surgery concludes. We aim to eliminate this subjective recall bias, translating exact visual ground-truth into flawless medical records.

Reducing Administrative Burden

Documentation is a leading cause of surgeon burnout. By automating report generation through a Vision-to-Language framework, we transform a passive administrative choke-point into an active, longitudinal clinical asset.

Harnessing the da Vinci SP

Robotic platforms like the da Vinci SP generate immense amounts of high-quality, stabilized 4K video. While this sheer volume of unstructured data is difficult to process manually, it provides the perfect, highly-controlled visual environment necessary to train an AI model.

40% of dictations omit critical intraoperative events compared to video ground-truth.

2 / Clinical Dataset & Constraints

The Stanford Dataset

Initiated in collaboration with Dr. Holsinger at Stanford University, our research leverages a unique, proprietary dataset generated directly by his clinical team. We secured 75 de-identified records containing synchronized 4K surgeries paired alongside human-dictated operative notes.

Scope & Surgical Target

We are strictly focused on Transoral Lateral Oropharyngectomy (TLO) procedures performed on the da Vinci SP platform. Because TLO dictates highly standardized technical stages, it serves as the perfect tightly-scoped baseline for aligning AI multimodal models before attempting to generalize to broader surgical methodologies.

3 / Data Pipeline Design

Processing raw surgical video requires an efficient and robust data pipeline. We developed a system that samples frames at 1Hz, applying lightweight preprocessing to normalize lighting and reduce artifact noise before downstream analysis.

Instead of analyzing every single frame, our pipeline uses a keyframe extraction module based on scene-change detection. This reduces computational overhead by 85% while preserving all critical visual information needed for phase recognition and instrument tracking.

4 / Model Architecture

Zero-Shot Classification

To identify distinct surgical phases without requiring massive annotated datasets, we implemented PeskaVLP—a vision-language model fine-tuned for the medical domain. We utilized a zero-shot classification approach, generating prompt embeddings for over 50 distinct surgical actions.

LLM Orchestration

The structured predictions from the vision model, including phase temporal boundaries tool presence, are passed as structured JSON into a large language model. Using a carefully constrained prompt strategy, the LLM converts this telemetry into a fluent, medically-accurate operative note.
