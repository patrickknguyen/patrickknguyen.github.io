Project Objective

With the adoption of the da Vinci SP at Stanford Medicine, Dr. Holsinger's team found themselves with an ever-growing stream of high-quality 4K surgical video. Dr. Holsinger identified the data as a largely underutilized asset and sought a research partnership to explore what was possible. ViGOR is a vision-language framework that automates operative note generation directly from surgical video.

Project Overview

The ViGOR pipeline ingests 4K surgical video from the da Vinci SP, applies image treatment and frame sampling, then classifies each segment into one of seven surgical phases using a vision encoder. Those keyframes then feed into a two-pass language model: the first pass generates per-frame clinical captions, and the second synthesizes them into a complete, structured operative note.

1 / Background & Motivation

Improving Clinical Accuracy

Operative notes written hours after procedure are subject to recall bias, omission, and compression. A system that reconstructs events directly from video produces a note anchored to visual evidence.

Reducing Administrative Burden

Documentation represents a significant and growing share of a surgeon's non-clinical time. Automating the first draft of an operative note could meaningfully reduce that burden, freeing capacity for direct patient care.

Harnessing the da Vinci SP

Robotic surgical systems such as the da Vinci SP generate immense amounts of stabilized 4K video. While this sheer volume of unstructured data is difficult to process manually, it provides the perfect, highly-controlled environment for computer vision research.

40% of dictations omit critical intraoperative events compared to video ground-truth.

2 / Dataset & Scope

The Stanford Dataset

Initiated in collaboration with Dr. Holsinger at Stanford University, our research leverages a dataset generated directly by his clinical team. The dataset includes 75 de-identified records containing synchronized 4K surgery videos alongside human-dictated operative notes.

Scope & Surgical Target

We are strictly focused on Transoral Lateral Oropharyngectomy (TLO) procedures performed on the da Vinci SP platform at a single institution, under a single surgical team. The tradeoff is limited generalizability. Results cannot be assumed to transfer to other procedures or platforms without revalidation.

3 / Data Pipeline Design

Raw surgical video cannot be fed directly into a language model. The data pipeline transforms 4K recordings into a curated set of labeled, visually informative keyframes ready for downstream processing.

Image Preprocessing

Raw endoscopic frames contain black pillarbox bars and low-contrast tissue regions that impair model performance. Frames are center-cropped to square and contrast-enhanced to improve feature visibility. Mosaic tiling was also explored as a method for presenting multiple frames within a single model input.

Sampling

Frames are sampled proportionally to phase duration rather than at a fixed interval — shorter phases receive fewer frames, ensuring the model's attention is distributed meaningfully across the full procedure rather than over-representing longer segments.

Phase Classification

Three frozen vision encoders were benchmarked using a Leave-One-Case-Out linear probe: PeskaVLP (surgical vision-language model), DINOv2 (general-purpose ViT), and LemonFM (surgical foundation model). The winning encoder's phase labels structure all downstream note generation.

4 / Model Architecture

Zero-Shot Classification

To identify distinct surgical phases without requiring massive annotated datasets, we implemented PeskaVLP—a vision-language model fine-tuned for the medical domain. We utilized a zero-shot classification approach, generating prompt embeddings for over 50 distinct surgical actions.

LLM Orchestration

The structured predictions from the vision model, including phase temporal boundaries tool presence, are passed as structured JSON into a large language model. Using a carefully constrained prompt strategy, the LLM converts this telemetry into a fluent, medically-accurate operative note.
